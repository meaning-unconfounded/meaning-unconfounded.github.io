Chapter 1

The night Dave became dangerous

It was Dave who started it. Three pints deep in the Nag's Head.

👔
Dave — Insurance, Unexpectedly Brilliant
"Why's it called a PINT? P-I-N-T. Project–Vessel–Continue–Mark. It's a projected vessel that continues to a mark. A measured container. Someone built the word like they built the glass."
🎓
Siobhan — Manchester, Skeptical
"That's not how language works, Dave. Words are arbitrary. Saussure. Convention."
👔
Dave
"Then why's a pint called a pint and not a GLORP?"
That, reader, is how I ended up spending six months in pubs with a notebook, decoding the architecture of English while my liver filed for divorce.
